# CrewShift — Agent Assets for Landing Page Design

Each agent gets a section on the landing page. Below are the creative assets for each: tagline, short description, long description, pain point it solves, before/after scenario, suggested icon/visual, feature bullets, and a sample AI interaction.

---

## 1. Invoice Agent

**Tagline:** "Jobs done. Money in."

**Short description (1 line):**
Automatically turns completed work into invoices, sends them, and tracks every dollar.

**Long description (2-3 lines):**
The invoice agent watches your completed jobs, generates accurate invoices with the right rates, markup, and tax, and delivers them to the right person in the format they expect. Residential gets an email. GCs get AIA pay applications with backup docs. Warranty companies get billed through their portal. You stop losing days between finishing the work and sending the bill.

**Pain point:**
"I finished a $4,800 job on Tuesday. Didn't send the invoice until the following Monday because I was too busy. That's a week of my money sitting in someone else's pocket."

**Before/After:**

| Before CrewShift | After CrewShift |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Invoices sent 3-7 days after job | Invoices sent same day |
| Manually calculating tax and markup | Rates, markup, tax applied automatically |
| Forgetting to bill completed jobs | Unbilled work flagged immediately |
| Sending a regular invoice to a GC | AIA G702/G703 generated with backup docs |
| No idea if a job was profitable | Margin calculated on every invoice |

**Suggested icon/visual:** Dollar sign with a checkmark, or an invoice document with a green "sent" indicator

**Feature bullets for cards/grid:**
- Same-day invoicing from completed jobs
- GC pay applications in AIA format
- Insurance and warranty company billing
- Automatic lien waiver generation
- Real-time margin per job
- Membership and recurring billing
- Unbilled revenue alerts
- Multi-entity and tax jurisdiction support

**Sample AI interaction:**
> **You:** "How much unbilled work do I have?"
> **CrewShift:** "You have 8 completed jobs with no invoice, totaling $14,200. The oldest is from 6 days ago at 312 Park Ave. Want me to generate all 8 invoices?"

## 2. Estimate Agent

**Tagline:** "Price it right. Win the job."

**Short description (1 line):**
Builds professional proposals in minutes, follows up automatically, and tracks every bid until it closes.

**Long description (2-3 lines):**
The estimate agent takes a job request, a photo, or a set of plans and produces a professional proposal with Good/Better/Best options, accurate pricing from your rate book, and a clear scope of work. It follows up with the customer on a schedule, tracks whether they opened it, and tells you when to call. When they say yes, it converts the estimate into a contract and kicks off scheduling, material ordering, and permits.

**Pain point:**
"A customer called Monday asking for a quote. I was on a job all week. By the time I sent the estimate Friday, they'd already hired someone else."

**Before/After:**

| Before CrewShift | After CrewShift |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Estimates take 2-5 days to send | Sent within minutes |
| One price, take it or leave it | Good/Better/Best options |
| No follow-up, hope they call back | Automated follow-up sequence |
| No idea why you lost the bid | Win/loss tracking with reasons |
| Change orders done on a handshake | Documented and approved before work starts |

**Suggested icon/visual:** Document with a price tag, or a proposal with tiered options (bronze/silver/gold)

**Feature bullets for cards/grid:**
- Proposals generated in minutes from job requests or photos
- Good/Better/Best tiered pricing
- Automatic follow-up sequences
- E-signature and deposit collection
- Estimate-to-contract conversion
- Change order management
- Plan takeoff for commercial bids
- Win/loss and pipeline tracking

**Sample AI interaction:**
> **You:** "I need an estimate for a water heater replacement at 45 Elm St."
> **CrewShift:** "Based on the property record, this is a 3-story residential. I've built 3 options: repair ($850), standard replacement ($3,200), and high-efficiency upgrade ($4,800). The high-efficiency qualifies for a federal tax credit. Want me to send it to the customer?"

**Feature bullets for cards/grid:**
- Smart tech-to-job matching (skills, certs, location)
- Job briefings with property history and customer notes
- Real-time schedule reshuffling
- Overtime monitoring and alerts
- Capacity planning and hiring signals

- Customer time window management

## 6. Compliance Agent

**Tagline:** "Stay legal. Stay working."

**Short description (1 line):**
Tracks every license, permit, violation, insurance policy, and deadline so nothing shuts you down.

**Long description (2-3 lines):**
The compliance agent monitors the full regulatory surface area of your business. Building violations, permit filings, inspections, contractor licenses, insurance policies, OSHA requirements, environmental regulations, contract obligations, and fleet compliance. It pulls data from city databases automatically, warns you before deadlines hit, pre-fills your permit forms, and prepares you for every hearing, inspection, and broker conversation. Starting with NYC, the hardest market in the country.

**Pain point:**
"I got a DOB violation and didn't even know about it until the penalty doubled. Then I missed the cure date and now I have a hearing."

**Before/After:**

| Before CrewShift | After CrewShift |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Find out about violations after the penalty doubles | Proactive monitoring catches violations the day they're filed |
| Manually tracking license expirations on a spreadsheet | Every license and cert tracked with 90/60/30 day warnings |
| Scrambling to figure out what permit you need | Permit type determined, forms pre-filled from your data |
| Insurance gaps discovered when GC rejects your bid | Gaps flagged before you bid, with broker talking points |
| Missing cure dates and cert of correction deadlines | Every deadline tracked with escalating reminders |

**Suggested icon/visual:** Shield with a checkmark, or a compliance checklist with status indicators

**Feature bullets for cards/grid:**
- Automatic violation detection from city databases
- Permit form pre-fill and filing guidance
- License and certification tracking for every team member
- Insurance policy monitoring with gap analysis
- Inspection prep checklists
- OSHA compliance tracking
- Contract obligation monitoring
- Weekly compliance digest
- Multi-jurisdiction support (NYC, LA, Chicago, more)

**Sample AI interaction:**
> **You:** "I just got a violation notice." [uploads photo]
> **CrewShift:** "This is a Class 2 DOB violation for plumbing work without a permit at 123 Main St. Cure date is April 15. Here's your action plan: (1) File for Alt-2 permit on DOB NOW by March 28. I've pre-filled the PW2 form with your license and property data. (2) Complete the work. (3) Schedule plumbing inspection. (4) File Certificate of Correction by April 15. Miss the date and this goes to OATH hearing with penalties up to $25,000."

## 7. Inventory Agent

**Tagline:** "The right part. On the truck. Every time."

**Short description (1 line):**
Tracks parts on every truck and in the warehouse, reorders before you run out, and finds the best price.

**Long description (2-3 lines):**
The inventory agent knows what's on each truck, what's in the warehouse, and what's needed for upcoming jobs. It sets reorder points that actually work based on your usage patterns. It compares pricing across your suppliers so you're not overpaying. When a job is scheduled, it checks if the tech has the parts. When they don't, it routes them to the nearest supply house or triggers a delivery.

**Pain point:**
"My tech drove 45 minutes to a job, didn't have the right valve, drove 30 minutes to the supply house, and charged the customer for 2 hours of nothing. That's $300 in wasted labor and one angry customer."

**Before/After:**

| Before CrewShift | After CrewShift |
|-----------------|----------------|
| No idea what's on each truck | Real-time truck inventory |
| Run out of common parts on the job | Reorder points based on actual usage |
| Paying whatever the first supplier quotes | Price comparison across vendors |
| Parts usage not tied to specific jobs | Every part tracked to the job it was used on |
| Emergency supply house runs killing productivity | Parts pre-staged based on tomorrow's schedule |

**Suggested icon/visual:** Box/package with a location pin, or a truck with an inventory checklist

**Feature bullets for cards/grid:**
- Truck-level inventory tracking
- Warehouse stock management
- Smart reorder points based on usage patterns
- Multi-vendor price comparison
- Parts pre-staging for scheduled jobs
- Purchase order generation
- Supplier relationship tracking
- Parts-to-job cost tracking

**Sample AI interaction:**

### Five-Tier Autonomy

**Tagline:** "You control how much AI does."

**Visual concept:** A slider or dial showing the five levels, from fully automatic to fully human-controlled.

| Tier | Label | What it means | Example |
|------|-------|--------------|---------|
| Auto | Runs without you | Background tasks that don't need approval | Syncing data, calculating margins, tracking deadlines |
| Communicate | Sends on your behalf | Routine messages that just need to go out | Appointment reminders, payment receipts, follow-up emails |
| Review | You approve it | AI drafts, you say yes or edit | Invoices, estimates, formal notices |
| Prepare | You take the meeting | AI assembles everything you need, you handle the conversation | Collection calls, GC negotiations, insurance broker meetings |
| Escalate | Needs you now | Something urgent or high-stakes that only a human can decide | Legal action, safety incidents, financial emergencies |

---

### Agent Chaining

**Tagline:** "Everything connected. Nothing siloed."

**Visual concept:** A flow diagram showing how one event triggers multiple agents.

**Example chain to visualize:**
```
Job Completed
  |
  +---> Invoice Agent (generates and sends invoice)
  |       |
  |       +---> Finance Agent (logs revenue, updates cash forecast)
  |       |
  |       +---> 30 days unpaid ---> Collections Agent (starts outreach)
  |
  +---> Inventory Agent (updates truck stock, triggers reorder)
  |
  +---> Customer Agent (sends review request, logs service history)
  |
  +---> Compliance Agent (triggers inspection if permit job)
```

**Another example:**
```
Violation Detected
  |
  +---> Compliance Agent (action plan, deadlines, permit prep)
  |
  +---> Field-Ops Agent (stop work order? reschedule affected jobs)
  |
  +---> Finance Agent (log fine exposure, update cash forecast)
  |
  +---> Customer Agent (flag property owner relationship)
```
