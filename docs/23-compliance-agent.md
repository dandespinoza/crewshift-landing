# Compliance Agent — Full Scope

The compliance agent manages the entire regulatory surface area of running a trades business. Not just violations. Everything that can shut you down, fine you, or lose you a job if you're not on top of it.

This is NOT a standalone agent. It chains into every other agent (field-ops, finance, collections, estimate, customer) because compliance touches everything.

CrewShift doesn't navigate the bureaucracy for you. It makes sure you know exactly what's required, prepares everything you need, and never lets a deadline slip. The human handles the relationships with inspectors, DOB offices, insurance brokers, and attorneys. The AI handles the tracking, preparation, and paperwork.

---

## The Core Job

A contractor has to stay legal, licensed, insured, permitted, and in good standing across dozens of overlapping regulatory systems. Miss one deadline and you get fined. Miss another and you can't pull permits. Miss another and your insurance lapses and you can't work. Most contractors track this in their head, on sticky notes, or not at all. The compliance agent tracks everything, warns early, prepares the paperwork, and makes sure nothing falls through.

## Capabilities

### 1. Building Violations

Reactive. Something went wrong.

- DOB violations (Class 1/2/3)
- ECB/OATH violations (fines, hearings, liens)
- Stop work orders
- Vacate orders
- Certificate of correction filings
- Violation dismissal tracking
- Repeat violation flagging (penalties double/triple)

### 2. Inspections

The scheduling, prep, pass/fail loop.

- Permit-required inspections (rough, final, special)
- Violation correction inspections
- Re-inspections after failure
- Special inspections (structural, concrete, welding, firestopping)
- Inspection result tracking and documentation
- Inspector prep checklists per inspection type

### 3. Permits & Filings

The paperwork before and during work.

- Work permits (NB, ALT-1/2/3, plumbing, electrical, mechanical)
- Permit renewals (they expire, most contractors forget)
- Permit amendments (scope changed mid-job)
- Certificate of Occupancy (TCO/final CO)
- Letters of completion
- Asbestos (ACP-5/ACP-7) filings for pre-1985 buildings
- Landmark Preservation Commission filings
- FDNY permits (gas, fire suppression, fire alarm, flammable storage)
- DOT permits (sidewalk, street, crane)
- DEP permits (sewer connection, backflow preventer)
- After-hours work permits

### 4. Insurance & Risk

- General Liability (GL)
- Workers Compensation (WC)
- Commercial Auto
- Umbrella/Excess
- Professional Liability (E&O)
- Builder's Risk
- Inland Marine / Tools & Equipment
- Pollution Liability
- Surety Bonds (bid, performance, payment)
- Disability Insurance (state-required)
- Paid Family Leave (state-required)

Tracks every policy: carrier, number, limits, expiry, premium. Knows what each GC/project requires. Flags gaps. Prepares COI requests for broker.

### 5. Contractor Licensing

Every person, every trade, every jurisdiction.

- Business license (city/county registration)
- Contractor license (master plumber, master electrician, etc.)
- Journeyman cards (per tech)
- EPA certifications (608, RRP)
- OSHA cards (OSHA-10, OSHA-30)
- Site Safety Training (SST)
- Scaffolding/rigging licenses
- Welding certifications (per welder, per position)
- Backflow preventer tester certification
- Fire Guard certificates (FDNY)
- Certificate of Fitness (FDNY)
- DOB Safety Registration
- State license

### 6. Vendor & Subcontractor Compliance

When you hire subs, you're liable for their compliance.

- Sub insurance verification (GL, WC, auto, limits)
- Sub license verification
- Sub safety record (EMR rating)
- W-9 / tax ID collection
- Lien waiver collection (conditional, unconditional, partial, final)
- Certified payroll (prevailing wage jobs)
- Sub agreement compliance (MSA signed?)
- M/WBE/DBE tracking (public projects)
- E-Verify compliance (federal projects)

### 7. Safety & OSHA Compliance

- OSHA 300 Log (recordable injuries)
- OSHA 300A Summary (annual posting Feb 1-Apr 30)
- OSHA 301 Incident Reports
- Site-specific safety plans
- Toolbox talks (weekly, documented)
- Job Hazard Analysis (JHA)
- SDS/MSDS sheets
- PPE tracking
- Fall protection plans
- Confined space entry permits
- Hot work permits
- Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures
- Excavation/trenching permits
- Crane/hoist operation logs
- Drug testing compliance
- Experience Modification Rate (EMR)

### 8. Environmental Compliance

- Asbestos (survey, abatement, monitoring, DEP notifications)
- Lead paint (EPA RRP rule, certified firm + renovator)
- Refrigerant handling (EPA 608, recovery, recordkeeping)
- Stormwater/erosion (SWPPP plans)
- Waste disposal (hazardous waste manifesting)
- Air quality permits (generators, boilers, spray booths)
- Noise ordinances (work hour restrictions)
- Dust control (NYC LL47, Community Air Survey)

### 9. Tax & Payroll Compliance

- Prevailing wage (public/government projects)
- Certified payroll (weekly submission)
- 1099 vs W-2 classification
- Sales tax on materials
- Use tax (out-of-state purchases)
- Payroll tax filings (941s, W-2s, 1099s)
- NYC commercial rent tax
- Union reporting (benefit fund reports)

### 10. Contract Compliance

- Milestone deadlines (liquidated damages)
- Change order procedures
- Notice requirements (written notice of delay, concealed conditions)
- Retainage tracking
- Closeout requirements (as-builts, O&M manuals, warranties, training)
- Warranty periods
- Indemnification obligations
- Insurance requirements per contract
- Bonding requirements
- M/WBE participation goals

### 11. Fleet & Equipment Compliance

- DOT vehicle inspections (annual)
- CDL medical cards (biennial)
- Vehicle registration renewals
- ELD compliance (vehicles over 10,001 lbs)
- Equipment inspections (crane, hoist, scaffold)
- Equipment certifications (calibration records)
- Fuel tax reporting (IFTA, cross-state)

### 12. Public/Government Project Compliance

- Prequalification (financial review, safety record)
- Bid compliance (bid bonds, non-collusion affidavit, buy-American)
- DBE/M/WBE goals
- Davis-Bacon / prevailing wage
- Project Labor Agreements (PLA)
- Monthly utilization reports
- VENDEX/PASSPort (NYC vendor registration)
- Responsible Bidder requirements
- Ethics/anti-corruption compliance

## Multi-Jurisdiction Architecture

All of the above varies by jurisdiction. The compliance agent uses an adapter pattern:

- One universal interface (violation, permit, license, deadline)
- One adapter per jurisdiction (NYC DOB, LA LADBS, Chicago Buildings, etc.)
- The service layer is jurisdiction-agnostic
- The AI prompts inject jurisdiction-specific context dynamically
- NYC, LA, Chicago, Miami-Dade all use Socrata open data APIs: one base client, different dataset IDs

If it works in NYC (the hardest market), it works everywhere.
