> Generated by the Product Design Team pipeline
> Project: CrewShift — AI Operations Platform for the Trades
> Date: 2026-03-04

## 1. Product Context (Phase 1: PM + UX Researcher)

### 1.1 Product Definition

**CrewShift** is a managed AI operations platform for the trades industry (plumbing, HVAC, electrical, general contractors). CrewShift staff onboard each client, connect integrations, and configure AI agents that automate dispatching, billing, communications, and inventory.

The **client dashboard** (`apps/web/`) is the daily workspace where trade business owners, office managers, and field supervisors manage their operations. It must feel fast, authoritative, and purpose-built — not like a generic SaaS template.

### 1.2 Target Personas

| Persona | Role | Context | Goals |
|---------|------|---------|-------|
| **Mike the Owner** | Business Owner, 45 | Runs a 12-person HVAC company. Checks dashboard on phone between jobs and on desktop in the evening. Not tech-savvy. | See revenue/job status at a glance, approve AI-recommended actions, trust the system. |
| **Sarah the Office Manager** | Office Admin, 32 | Lives in the dashboard 8hrs/day. Manages scheduling, invoicing, customer comms. Power user. | Fast data entry, quick navigation, zero friction for repetitive tasks. |
| **Carlos the Field Lead** | Field Supervisor, 38 | Checks mobile dashboard on job sites. Needs job details, customer info, route info. | Quick access to today's jobs, customer contact info, job notes. |

### 1.3 User Stories

1. **As Mike**, I want to see my business health in 5 seconds so that I can make decisions without digging through reports.
2. **As Sarah**, I want to navigate between jobs, invoices, and customers with zero delay so that I can handle 50+ tasks per day without fatigue.
3. **As Sarah**, I want to review and approve AI agent actions in bulk so that the system can operate semi-autonomously with my oversight.
4. **As Carlos**, I want to see today's job details on my phone so that I can prepare for each service call.
5. **As Mike**, I want the AI copilot to answer questions about my business in plain language so that I don't need to learn complex reporting tools.

### 1.4 Success Criteria

- **Time to insight**: Dashboard loads key metrics in < 2 seconds
- **Navigation efficiency**: Any page reachable in ≤ 2 clicks from any other page
- **Task completion rate**: 95%+ for core workflows (create job, send invoice, approve agent action)
- **Mobile usability**: All critical flows work on 375px viewport
- **Perceived speed**: All transitions feel < 400ms (Doherty Threshold)

### 1.5 Cognitive Principles Applied

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hick's Law** | Sidebar navigation limited to 7 items. Settings uses tabs (3) not a mega-menu. Agent review uses binary approve/reject. |
| **Fitts's Law** | Primary CTAs (New Job, New Invoice, Approve) are large (44px+ height), positioned top-right where the eye lands after reading the page title. Destructive actions (Reject, Delete) are smaller and separated. |
| **Miller's Law** | Dashboard shows exactly 4 stat cards. Data tables chunk information with clear column headers. Agent cards show 3 key metrics max. |
| **Jakob's Law** | Left sidebar navigation (Jira, Linear, Notion convention). Top header with search + profile right. Data tables follow spreadsheet mental model. |
| **Doherty Threshold** | Skeleton screens for all data loads. Optimistic UI for approve/reject. Page transitions animated at 200-300ms. |

### 1.6 Accessibility Requirements (WCAG 2.2 AA)

- **Contrast**: All text meets 4.5:1 minimum. Large text (18px+ bold) meets 3:1. Orange accent (#FF751F) on white fails AA for small text — use on large text/icons or with dark backgrounds only.
- **Keyboard**: Full tab navigation. Visible focus rings on all interactive elements. Escape closes modals. Arrow keys for tab groups.
- **Screen readers**: Proper heading hierarchy (h1 per page, h2 sections, h3 subsections). ARIA labels on icon-only buttons. Live regions for toast notifications and real-time updates.
- **Touch targets**: Minimum 44x44px on mobile, 32x32px on desktop.
- **Motion**: Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. No auto-playing animations. All transitions < 300ms.

### 1.7 Usability Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Orange accent (#FF751F) on white background fails WCAG AA for small text (3.3:1 ratio) | Use orange only on large text (18px+ bold), as background fills with white text, or as decorative indicators. Never for body-text-sized links. |
| Data tables overwhelming on mobile | Responsive: show 2-3 priority columns on mobile, hide rest behind horizontal scroll or detail view |
| AI agent actions need trust signals | Show confidence %, reasoning summary, and easy undo for approved actions |
| Too many settings tabs could grow | Progressive disclosure — show Integrations by default, collapse less-used tabs |

## 2. Brand & Visual Foundation (Phase 2: UI Manager)

### 2.1 Brand Profile Dials

| Dial | Value | Rationale |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **Temperature** | `neutral` | Trades industry demands authority and no-nonsense clarity. Cool feels too corporate/fintech, warm feels consumer-friendly. Pure neutral communicates industrial reliability. |
| **Contrast** | `crisp` | Trade workers need instant readability in varying conditions (office fluorescent lights, truck cab, job site). Crisp borders and clear surface separation reduce cognitive load. |
| **Radius** | `sharp` (6px) | Industrial-refined aesthetic. Sharp corners communicate precision and professionalism. Rounded/pill would soften the brand too much for construction/trades. |
| **Shadow** | `subtle` | Minimal shadow for card elevation. Too much shadow feels decorative; none feels flat/unfinished. Subtle shadows give depth without distraction. |
| **Density** | `normal` | Power users (Sarah) need enough density to see data, but owners (Mike) need breathing room. Normal strikes the balance. Tables can be slightly denser. |
| **Accent personality** | `vivid` | The orange (#FF751F) IS the brand. It must pop — like a safety vest on a job site. High chroma, unapologetic. It earns attention. |
| **Accent usage** | `standard` | Orange for primary buttons, active nav, key badges, selected states, and status indicators. NOT for tinted surfaces or backgrounds. The 60-30-10 rule: white dominant, black/gray secondary, orange accent only. |

### 2.2 Signature Move

**Choice: #3 — Table Style: Strong Dividers**

**Rationale**: CrewShift is a data-heavy operations platform. Tables are the primary interaction surface for Sarah (office manager) who spends 8 hours in the dashboard. Strong, clean horizontal dividers with a subtle header treatment make data scannable and give CrewShift a distinctive "operations control panel" feel. This differentiates from the soft/borderless tables common in generic SaaS templates.

Additional accent: The sidebar uses a **left border indicator** (3px orange) on the active nav item — this is a consistent "shift" motif that echoes the logo's angular geometry.

### 2.5 Typography

**Font Family**: **Red Hat Display** — used throughout for both headings and body text.

Red Hat Display is a geometric sans-serif with a strong, modern personality. Its slightly wide letterforms and clean geometry communicate industrial precision while remaining highly readable. Available weights: 300 (Light), 400 (Regular), 500 (Medium), 600 (SemiBold), 700 (Bold), 800 (ExtraBold), 900 (Black).

| Usage | Weight | Tracking | Line Height |
|-------|--------|----------|-------------|
| Display / Page Titles | 700 (Bold) | -0.025em | 1.1 |
| Section Headings (h2) | 600 (SemiBold) | -0.02em | 1.2 |
| Subsection Headings (h3) | 600 (SemiBold) | -0.01em | 1.25 |
| Body Text | 400 (Regular) | 0 | 1.5 |
| Body Emphasis | 500 (Medium) | 0 | 1.5 |
| Table Headers | 500 (Medium) | 0.03em (uppercase) | 1.4 |
| Captions / Metadata | 400 (Regular) | 0.01em | 1.4 |
| Buttons | 500 (Medium) | 0.01em | 1 |

**Type Scale** (Minor Third — 1.200 ratio, base 15px):

| Step | Size | Use |
|------|------|-----|
| xs | 12px | Badges, fine print, table metadata |
| sm | 13px | Captions, helper text, timestamps |
| base | 15px | Body text, table cells, form labels |
| lg | 18px | Large body, card titles, list headers |

**Why Minor Third at 15px**: Dashboard UIs need subtle hierarchy without huge size jumps. 15px base is slightly larger than 14px (better readability) without feeling oversized. Minor Third (1.2) keeps the scale tight for dense data views while still creating visual hierarchy.

## 3. Information Architecture & Flows (Phase 3: UX Manager)

### 3.1 View Structure

```
CrewShift Client Dashboard
├── Auth
│   ├── /login          — Email/password sign-in
│   └── /signup         — New account registration
│
├── Dashboard Shell (sidebar + header)
│   ├── /dashboard      — Business overview (stats, activity, quick actions)
│   ├── /jobs           — Job management (list → detail)
│   │   └── /jobs/[id]  — Job detail (future)
│   ├── /invoices       — Invoice tracking (list → detail)
│   │   └── /invoices/[id] — Invoice detail (future)
│   ├── /customers      — Customer management (list → detail)
│   │   └── /customers/[id] — Customer detail (future)
│   ├── /agents         — AI agent control panel + review queue
│   ├── /copilot        — AI chat interface
│   └── /settings       — Account settings
│       ├── Integrations tab
│       ├── Team tab
│       └── Notifications tab
```

### 3.2 Navigation Hierarchy

**Primary Navigation (Sidebar — always visible on desktop)**:
1. Dashboard (home icon)
2. Jobs (briefcase icon)
3. Invoices (file-text icon)
4. Customers (users icon)
5. Agents (bot icon)
6. Copilot (message-square icon)
7. Settings (settings icon)

**Utility Navigation (Header)**:
- Search (global, keyboard shortcut Cmd+K)
- Notifications bell (with unread count badge)
- User avatar/menu (profile, preferences, sign out)

**Active State Treatment**:
- Sidebar: 3px left border in `accent.600` + `accent.50` background tint + text in `accent.600`
- All other items: `text.secondary`, no background, no border

### 3.3 Key User Flows

#### Flow 1: Dashboard → Quick Insight (Mike — 5 seconds)
1. Land on `/dashboard`
2. See 4 stat cards with key metrics (animated count-up on load)
3. Scan "Recent Jobs" for any issues (status badges catch the eye)
4. Scan "Agent Activity" for pending approvals (orange badges)
5. If pending approvals → click to navigate to `/agents`

#### Flow 2: Create Invoice (Sarah — under 30 seconds)
1. Navigate to `/invoices` via sidebar
2. Click "New Invoice" button (top-right, primary CTA)
3. Modal/slide-over opens with form
4. Select customer (searchable dropdown)
5. Add line items
6. Click "Create" → optimistic UI shows invoice immediately
7. Toast confirms: "Invoice #1042 created"

#### Flow 3: Review Agent Actions (Sarah — batch process)
1. Navigate to `/agents`
2. See Review Queue section with pending items
3. Each item shows: agent name, action description, confidence %, priority
4. Click "Approve" → item slides out with animation, counter updates
5. Click "Reject" → brief reason required → item removed
6. Batch select possible for bulk approve

#### Flow 4: AI Copilot Query (Mike — natural language)
1. Navigate to `/copilot`
2. See suggestion chips for common questions
3. Type or click suggestion: "What's my revenue this month?"
4. Copilot streams response with formatted data
5. Can follow up: "Compare to last month"

### 3.4 Empty State Strategy

| View | Empty State |
|------|-------------|
| Dashboard (new account) | Welcome message + setup checklist (connect integrations, invite team, create first job) |
| Jobs (no jobs) | Illustration + "Create your first job" CTA + helper text about importing from ServiceTitan/Jobber |
| Invoices (no invoices) | "No invoices yet" + "Create Invoice" CTA |
| Agent Review Queue (empty) | Checkmark icon + "All caught up! No pending reviews." |
| Copilot (first use) | Welcome message with 4 suggestion chips |

### 3.5 Gestalt Principles Applied

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Proximity** | Stats cards in a tight 4-column grid with 16px gaps. Form field labels directly above inputs with 4px gap. Related table columns grouped visually. |
| **Similarity** | All nav items share identical height/padding/font. All data table rows share identical height. All status badges share same size but differ by color. |
| **Continuity** | Sidebar items aligned on a single vertical axis. Table columns on strict grid. Stat card values aligned vertically across the row. |
| **Common Region** | Cards with border create distinct regions. Sidebar is a visually separate panel. Header is a separate bar. Each settings tab is a separate region. |
| **Figure-Ground** | Modals float above a semi-transparent overlay (`rgba(0,0,0,0.5)`). Dropdowns elevated with `shadow.2`. Sidebar visually separated from main content. |

### 3.6 Responsive Strategy

| Breakpoint | Width | Layout Changes |
|------------|-------|---------------|
| `mobile` | < 640px | Sidebar collapses to hamburger menu. Stats cards stack 2x2. Tables show 2-3 columns. Header search hidden. |
| `tablet` | 640-1024px | Sidebar persists as a narrow icon-only rail (64px). Stats cards 2x2. Tables show 4-5 columns. |
| `desktop` | > 1024px | Full sidebar (264px). Stats cards 4-column. Full tables. Two-column layouts (dashboard). |

**Mobile-First Rules**:
- All touch targets minimum 44x44px
- Bottom-fixed action bar for mobile table views (bulk actions)
- Swipe-to-dismiss on mobile for toasts
- Copilot input fixed at bottom of viewport on mobile

### 3.7 Animation & Transition Strategy

| Interaction | Animation | Duration | Easing |
|-------------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Page transition | Fade + subtle slide up (8px) | 200ms | `ease-out` |
| Sidebar hover | Background fade in | 150ms | `ease` |
| Card hover | Slight Y translate (-2px) + shadow increase | 200ms | `ease-out` |
| Button press | Scale down (0.97) | 100ms | `ease-in-out` |
| Modal open | Fade in + scale from 0.95 | 250ms | `spring(1, 80, 10)` |
| Modal close | Fade out + scale to 0.95 | 150ms | `ease-in` |
| Toast enter | Slide in from right | 300ms | `spring(1, 80, 12)` |
| Toast exit | Fade out + slide right | 200ms | `ease-in` |
| Table row hover | Background color fade | 150ms | `ease` |
| Stats card load | Count-up animation | 600ms | `ease-out` (staggered 100ms each) |
| Review item approve | Slide left + fade | 300ms | `ease-in-out` |
| Review item reject | Shake briefly (3px) → slide out | 400ms | `ease-in-out` |
| Nav item active | Left border grows from center | 200ms | `ease-out` |
| Skeleton pulse | Opacity 0.4 → 0.7 loop | 1500ms | `ease-in-out` |
| Dropdown open | Fade + slide down (4px) | 200ms | `ease-out` |
| Tab switch | Content crossfade | 200ms | `ease` |
| Badge count update | Brief scale pulse (1.2→1) | 300ms | `spring` |

**Animation Principles**:
- All animations respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — disable non-essential motion
- Entrances: `ease-out` (fast start, gentle landing — feels responsive)
- Exits: `ease-in` (gentle start, fast exit — gets out of the way)
- Interactive: `ease-in-out` or spring (feels physical, tactile)
- Stagger children by 50-100ms for lists/grids (feels intentional, not simultaneous)
- Never animate layout shifts that cause content reflow

### 5.1 Anti-Template Checklist

- [x] **Signature Move chosen and consistent** — Strong table dividers applied to ALL data tables (Jobs, Invoices, Customers). Header row gets `surface.bg1` background with uppercase, tracked-out labels.
- [x] **Accent usage style matches brand** — Standard. Orange for primary buttons, active nav (3px left border), key badges, status indicators. No tinted surface panels.
- [x] **Neutral temperature applied** — Pure neutral (no warm/cool tint). Industrial precision.
- [x] **Radius + density consistent** — 6px sharp throughout. Normal density (40px buttons, 48px table rows, 20-24px card padding).
- [x] **Card separation consistent** — Border + subtle shadow combo. All cards use `border.default` + `shadow.1`.
- [x] **Token schema used** — No raw hex in component specs. All reference token names.

### 5.2 Brand Personality Verification

**Does this feel like CrewShift, or generic SaaS?**

✅ **CrewShift identity markers:**
- The 3px left-border active indicator in the sidebar echoes the angular "shift" motif in the logo
- Strong table dividers communicate "operations control panel" — not a pretty marketing dashboard
- The vivid orange is unapologetic and industrial, like a safety vest on a job site
- Red Hat Display is distinctive — its slightly wide, geometric letterforms feel engineered, not decorative
- Sharp 6px radius says "precision tool" not "friendly consumer app"
- Count-up animations on stats cards make the dashboard feel alive without being playful

**What makes this different from the example profiles?**
- Not Fintech/Calm (no cool tones, no soft radius)
- Not Dev Tool/Crisp (not compact density, has personality via orange)
- Not Creator/Friendly (no pill radius, no warm tones, no airy shadows)
- CrewShift is its own profile: **Industrial-Precision** — neutral temperature, crisp contrast, sharp radius, vivid accent, standard usage

### 5.3 Dark Mode Readiness

- [x] Double-distance rule followed (6% jumps between bg0→bg1→bg2→bg3)
- [x] Accent steps adjusted (300-400 for dark mode buttons vs 600-700 for light)
- [x] Borders brightened (#3A3A42 vs #D1D3D9)
- [x] Text dimmed (#EDEDEF not pure #FFFFFF)
- [x] Semantic colors noted for dark mode testing

### 5.4 Final Critique & Improvements

1. **Orange accessibility on white**: The spec correctly identifies that `#FF751F` on white fails WCAG AA for small text (3.3:1). Using `accent.600` (#E5641A) for text links would be slightly better (3.7:1) but still borderline. **Recommendation**: For any orange text on white, use `accent.800` (#A84311) which passes AA (7.3:1). Reserve the vivid orange for fills, icons, and large display text only.

2. **Table density for Sarah (power user)**: The normal-density 48px row height is great for readability but Sarah processes 50+ rows daily. **Recommendation**: Add a user-controlled density toggle in the table header (Compact: 40px / Normal: 48px) so power users can choose. Default to normal.

3. **Copilot response streaming**: The spec defines message styles but doesn't address streaming text appearance. **Recommendation**: Use a typing cursor animation (blinking `|` at end of streamed text) and render markdown progressively. This makes the AI feel responsive and alive.

## 6. Team Decision Log

### Debate 1: Font Choice — Red Hat Display for Everything?

**UX Researcher**: "Using one font for both headings and body is risky — usually you pair a display font with a text font for optimal readability at small sizes."
**UI Manager**: "Red Hat Display is designed with two optical sizes (Display and Text), but the user explicitly requested Red Hat Display throughout. Its x-height and letterform clarity are good enough for 15px body text."
**PM**: "Brand consistency matters for the trades audience. One font = one identity. Keep it simple."
**Head of Design**: "**Decision: Use Red Hat Display throughout.** The font's weight range (300-900) provides enough hierarchy. At 15px body size with 400 weight and 1.5 line height, it reads well. We compensate with strong weight contrast between hierarchy levels (400 body → 600 headings → 700 titles)."

### Debate 2: Shadow Model — None vs Subtle?

**UI Manager**: "Crisp brands often use `none` shadow with border-only separation."
**Staff Designer**: "Cards without shadows on a white background can feel flat and unstructured, especially with the amount of data CrewShift shows."
**UX Researcher**: "Shadow provides important figure-ground separation per Gestalt principles. Without it, the page becomes a wall of bordered rectangles."
**Head of Design**: "**Decision: Subtle shadow.** `shadow.1` (very light) combined with `border.default` gives enough elevation to separate cards without being decorative. The shadow model stays restrained — no airy/floaty effects."

### Debate 3: Accent Usage — Standard vs Expressive?

**PM**: "The orange IS the brand. Shouldn't we use it more aggressively? Tinted panels, highlighted sections?"
**UX Researcher**: "Research shows high-chroma accents used too broadly cause visual fatigue. Sarah stares at this 8 hours/day."
**UI Manager**: "The 60-30-10 rule caps accent at 10-15%. Standard usage puts orange exactly where it matters — CTAs and active states."
**Head of Design**: "**Decision: Standard usage.** The vivid chroma already makes the orange pop. Using it sparingly makes each orange element more impactful. Buttons, active nav, badges — that's enough. Background tints only for `accent.50` in selected states."

### Debate 4: Animation Level — How Much Is Too Much?

**PM**: "Mike (business owner) wants the dashboard to feel alive and modern. Animations signal quality."
**UX Researcher**: "Carlos (field lead) is on a job site with spotty connectivity. Heavy animations on slow devices frustrate users."
**Staff Designer**: "Framer Motion lets us animate layout changes and page transitions without performance cost if we use `layoutId` and avoid expensive spring configs."
**Head of Design**: "**Decision: Purposeful animation only.** Every animation must communicate state change (enter, exit, success, error) or create spatial continuity (page transitions). No decorative animation. All respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Count-up on stats cards and slide-out on review queue approvals are the signature micro-interactions."
